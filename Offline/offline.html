RUN FOR YOUR CODE

BVPIEEE Code-X brings to you the event in its annual technical festival, Fervour. It consists of 3 rounds, all rounds being conducted in C Block Labs, 3rd floor.
Participation is allowed for lone wolves or in a team of maximum 2 heads.



FIRST ROUND: Each participating team will be given 2 questions, one question to be coded on compiler(compiler provided is turbo c++) in a time limit of 10 minutes. And a code that has to be dry run on paper in a time limit of 3 minutes. The team that finishes with the round in given time limit and correctly, qualifies for the next round.
SECOND ROUND: 10 teams will qualify for this round. The qualifying team gets a code to be run on compiler, already fed into the computer. This code will contain syntactical and logical bugs that need to be corrected in a time limit of 20 minutes.
THIRD ROUND: 6 teams qualify for this round, once they debug the code in previous round in minimum time. In this round, a question will be provided that has to be coded on the compiler in minimum time. The correctly coded program and in minimum time, wins.
There are two runner ups and a winner.
Exciting cash prizes to be won :D

Follow Me

A line following robot is one which trails the line on the path it navigates. In this event the robots follow a white line on a black surface or vice versa (will depend on the level). The contest will have TWO ROUNDS. In the first round, the bots will be pitted in a race against eeach other, where the fastest bot wins. In the second round, the robot will have to navigate several turns at different angles and shapes. The robot which is able to navigate the track in shortest time will be declared the winner.

Problem Statement:
This is a line follower event. The task in this event is to build an autonomous bot that completes the course in the shortest period of time accurately tracking the black course line from start to finish over the white base.

Specifications:

Robot and arena specifications:

1. The width of the line will be 2cm.
2. The starting and end points will be visibly marked on the track itself.
3. The bot should cover the track according to the arrows drawn on the arena itself.
4. Ready-made sensors are allowed.
5. For teams which do not have an on-board supply, sockets will be provided for their adapters (the teams have to bring their own adapters). A small extension cord MAY be provided. In case it is not sufficient the teams will have to manage on their own.
6. Arena will be placed at the ground level.

Dimensions and Weights:
Bot must not exceed 250mm x 250mm x 250mm as l x b x h in dimensions at all times

Batteries and Power:

1. Each team must have their own batteries to power their bots.
2. The battery will be taken into consideration for the measurement to be made for the bot dimension.
3. Voltage must not exceed 24 volts between any two points.

General Rules:

1. Each team can have a maximum of 4 participants.
2. A team may consist of students from different colleges. Each member of the team must carry his/her I-card.
3. Certificates of Excellence will be awarded to the top three teams.
4. Bots should not be disassembled until the results are declared.
5. The organizers reserve the right to change the rules as they deem fit.
6. The team that finishes the track in minimum possible time will be the winner.
7. All students with a valid identity card of their respective educational institutes are eligible to participate in the event.
8. Judges decision will be final and binding to all.

Rules for the Bot:

1. The robot shall be self-contained (no remote controls).
2. The bot can use on-board power or off-board power supply.
3. The bot must be started by only one switch. However, a team can have a separate switch for restart.

4. A maximum of 3 restarts will be given to the teams. Every time a team opts for a restart, the bot will be restarted by placing the bot back to the starting position.
5. A robot shall not leave any part of its body behind while negotiating the path.
6. A robot shall not skip, scratch, mark, damage or destroy the lines of path.
7. The teams are not allowed to replace parts in between runs. However, defective parts can be replaced but with a penalty added to it.
8. A sample track will be given to teams for calibration of sensor and testing of their bots.
9. Any violation of these rules will constitute immediate disqualification from the contest and ineligibility for the associated prizes.
10. The machine will be checked for its safety before the race and will be discarded if found unsafe for other participants and spectators.
11. The organizers reserve the rights to change any of the rules as they deem fit.
12. The track of round 2 will be disclosed only 20 minutes before the start of round. The teams will have to manage accordingly (burning and editing of code should be done in that time only)

Rounds:

The competition may consist of two rounds

• The Prelims: All registered Teams. There will be two parallel tracks. Two teams will be competiting to finish the track. The first team to finish the track will be the winner.
• The Finals: Teams that qualify the prelims. This round will be time based. The team to finish the track in minimum possible time will be the winner.
